Yang Liu

My research focuses on two main areas: (1) embodied foundation models, with an emphasis on Vision-Language-Action models that integrate spatial perception, physical reasoning, intention understanding, capability awareness, and verifiable robot skill execution; and (2) human-centric generative AI and deployable visual intelligence, with the goal of creating scalable, controllable, and reliable systems for identity-preserving generation, digital humans, face analysis, and real-world visual perception. My research lies at the intersection of computer vision, multimodal foundation models, robotics, reinforcement learning, and real-world AI systems, striving to build embodied agents that can understand, act, learn, and safely improve in physical environments.

Previously, I developed large-scale visual perception and human-centric AIGC systems at Alibaba DAMO Academy and Tongyi Lab. My work on MogFace has achieved long-standing top performance on the WIDER FACE benchmark, while FaceChain, an identity-preserving portrait generation framework, received 9.5K+ GitHub stars and broad open-source impact. More recently, I have advanced embodied VLMs and industrial VLA systems for spatial understanding, scalable reinforcement learning, and robotic welding, aiming to bridge semantic reasoning and reliable physical action.
